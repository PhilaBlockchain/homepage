Pila Blockchain

Inspiring a healthy lifestyle for all South Africans using the power of blockchain.


What is the Pila Blockchain?

The Phila blockchain is a partially decentralized longitudinal health records system on the Ethereum blockchain with its own native transaction tokens and wallet; accessible across personal computing and mobile devices platforms. The Phila blockchain is an extension of the South African National Department of Health’s Phila campaign.

Key Features

public

Decentralised

All patient information is stored on a consortium of trusted nodes, meaning there is no central point of failure. These nodes have a vested interest in the success of the network.

book

Smart Contracts

Once deployed, each smart contract executes and records all healthcare transactions, interactions and events autonomously. No central authority can change the contracts.

lock

Immutability

The immutable nature of the blockchain ensures that once created, no patient master index identity can ever be altered, tampered, duplicated in any way by anyone; The data is forever.

search

Auditability

All smart contract interactions are recorded on the blockchain. Resultingly, any consortium member can verify the correctness of contract execution to ensure data integrity.

fingerprint

Privacy

Each patient on the network owns the private keys to their data. Resulting, they can control who has access to it, providing an unparalleled level of patient confidentiality.

Opensource

Phila blockchain aligns entirely with the South African government’s preference for open source solutions, and is entirely free to use. All code will remain free and open; forever.

How the Pila Works

The Phila Blockchain will securely store and manage encrypted longitudinal patient health records, and reward the patients as well as health care providers for uploading correct patient data. This system incentivizes/rewards patients and users for completing healthcare tests, preventative health tasks, behavior change, treatment initiation, treatment adherence and living healthier lifestyles. An accompanying mobile application will enable people to interact with their Phila account, viewing their health status and other key metric. Online interfaces will be provided for clinics and other medical practitioners.

Why We need Pila

The Phila blockchain network is designed to digitize and complement the Phila campaign by the South African government’s Department of Health, government departments’ and its partners’; which is aimed at inspiring people to make the 'right moves' when it comes to their health and lifestyle choices. It provides people with knowledge about their health and directs them to health and wellness services and facilities available to them. It also encourages knowledge of one’s health status, increased physical activity, healthier eating, adherence to treatment, good sexual health and a safe, non-violent society.

The Campaign aims to contribute to reducing the impacts of Non-Communicable Diseases, HIV and AIDS, TB, STI’s, Violence Injury and Trauma, Mother-Child-Woman Health epidemics.

The South African Healthcare Industry

South Africa's Public Healthcare sector is faced with problems of insufficient funding, lack of infrastructure, technology, supply chain inefficiency just to name a few. With various initiatives and campaigns under the Department of Health, Sustainable Development Goals, National Development Plan, National Strategic Plan and National Health Insurance; the South African Department of Health has converged collective goals from such policies under the PHILA campaign that seeks to promote holistic healthcare via a Prevention, Treatment, Care & Support approach. This approach is set to have major implications to the healthcare system which if fully implemented will result in less burden to the healthcare system and better allocation of the budget.

The Phila healthcare campaign specifically targets four highways of disease in the South African context, which are HIV & AIDS, Tuberculosis, Non-Communicable Diseases, Mother, Child, Women & Adolescent Health as well as Violence, Injuries & Trauma. The PHILA campaign relies on all traditional and social media platforms to communicate and engage the populace on the benefits of engaging in the campaign and targeted health outcomes. The PHILA Campaign’s objective is to move South Africans to act on their health status through prevention, treatment, care & support.

We propose a scalable Healthcare Blockchain solution, customized to the specifics of the PHILA Campaign and disease highways in the South African context.


This solution will digitize the Phila campaign and enable accountability, project management, progress tracking, audit trail, monitoring and evaluation of the campaign’s public health outcomes on an immutable and secure ledger on the blockchain. The Phila healthcare blockchain system will empower Healthcare Officials with a real-time overview and audit trail of the Phila campaign, as well as the ability to identify public healthcare threats and mitigate with appropriate healthcare interventions and solutions in precise geo-locations. For the generic population, the system will encourage and incentivize user adoption, preventative healthcare action, behavior change and desired health outcomes through reward incentives (through the issuance of a Phila token) upon completion of set healthcare goals such as testing, treatment, medicine/treatment adherence and achieving peer health champion status.

Whereas the current program uses online mentions - retweets, shares, likes, video - to track engagement and thus improved lifestyles, we will primarily use tokens as rewards, app sign ups, token transactions and other crypto-economic metrics to track progress of the program.

Problem Statements

Pila has identifed a series of problems with the current healthcare system. These issues effect all members of the system: the Department of Public Health, Patients and healthcare providers.

Faults in the current system

  • Patients' health information is stored in silos across multiple healthcare providers
  • Limited or lack of data integration creates gaps in patient health records and histories.
  • Patients repeatedly fill out the same basic health information for each new provider.
  • Patients have little to no access to real time personal health records and indicators
  • High rates of treatment default by patients on chronic medications with little to no adherence strategies
  • Ineffective incentives to encourage healthier lifestyle choices, preventative healthcare and behavior change
  • Ineffective electronic healthcare systems for tracking and measuring effects of public health campaigns

Funding Model

There will be a commercial token called RIBBON which will be taken to a token sale. Proceeds from the Token sale for Ribbon will be used to fund the development of the Phila blockchain platform and to make it scale. Token holders will be able to utilize the Ribbon tokens to transact on the data layer of the Phila blockchain to access health data without revealing patient identities. This will be made possible by the private and public key cryptography pair which the blockchain offers. All patients/users/clients of the Phila blockchain have this pair. The NDOH, as well as provincial and municipal departments of health will be the majority token holders and issuers at this level and will be able to see and predict health conditions nationwide, provincially or even locally. These tokens can also be traded on a secondary exchange by the majority holders (government) which could constitute a revenue stream to fund government budget deficits currently a huge problem in South Africa. Furthermore, funding healthcare in this era of shrinking funding for public health, NGOs, USAID/PEPFAR etc. is critical for South Africa and Africa as a whole.

The Phila tokens which are the tokens for the general public will however not go to a token sale. These tokens will be created solely by patients/users/clients having improved health indicators from their tests. Users and/or district/sub-district health departments will be the nodes in this system. Partnerships will be sought with retailers and other private sector partners for token holders to be able to redeem their tokens for various goods and services. In addition, there will also be an exchange where token holders can exchange their tokens for fiat currencies but this will be greatly underplayed and come at later stages. A mechanism will be created for management and issuance of these tokens by a foundation which will be managed by NDOH, Treasury and all other stakeholders.
